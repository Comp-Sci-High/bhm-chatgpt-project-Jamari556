Prominent Civil Rights Leaders

Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was a key leader in the American civil rights movement. He advocated for nonviolent resistance and delivered the iconic "I Have a Dream" speech. His efforts led to the Civil Rights Act of 1964.

Rosa Parks

Rosa Parks is best known for refusing to give up her bus seat to a white passenger in Montgomery, Alabama, in 1955. Her act of defiance sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott, a pivotal event in the civil rights movement.

Malcolm X

Malcolm X was a powerful advocate for Black empowerment and civil rights. As a prominent figure in the Nation of Islam, he promoted self-determination and later embraced a broader fight against racial injustice.
