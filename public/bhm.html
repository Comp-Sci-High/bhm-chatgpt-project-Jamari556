What is Black History Month?

Black History Month is an annual celebration recognizing the achievements and contributions of African Americans throughout history. It was officially recognized in 1976 by the United States government and is observed every February. The month serves as a time to honor the legacy of Black leaders, activists, and cultural pioneers who have shaped history.

The origins of Black History Month date back to 1926 when historian Carter G. Woodson launched "Negro History Week" to highlight Black contributions. Over time, this expanded into a month-long observance, promoting awareness, education, and appreciation of Black history and culture.
